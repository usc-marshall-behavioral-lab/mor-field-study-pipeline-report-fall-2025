Key finding

The semesters with the fastest time-to-50% (Fall 2024: 4 days, Spring 2025: 3 days) also exhibited the highest focal employee conversion rates. In Fall 2025, the submission window was held open longer, which increased student participation but delayed distribution into late November, compressing the response window into the end of the semester.

Response concentration by week

In the best-performing semesters, the majority of responses arrived in the first 1–2 weeks after launch. Fall 2025's batched distribution created two visible spikes (Nov 24 and Dec 3) rather than a natural decay curve.

The Fall 2024 signal

Fall 2024 submitted 62% fewer triads than Fall 2023 yet produced a comparable number of FE responses (157 vs 154) and more CW and SV responses (136 vs 127, 133 vs 128). Its 80.5% focal employee conversion rate was more than triple Fall 2025's 25.3%. Across semesters, fewer submissions combined with faster distribution has been associated with higher per-triad conversion rates.

What changed

For Spring 2026, the Step 2B submission form was updated with explicit language requiring legitimate company email addresses — no personal accounts (Gmail, Yahoo, WeChat, etc.). The screening questions also now route students through a readiness check before they can submit contacts. The result is the highest all-work-email rate and lowest all-personal rate in the study's history.

Why this matters

Higher domain matching rates are consistent with students submitting contacts who work at the same organization, reducing the likelihood of unrelated triads. The 20+ percentage-point increase in Spring 2026 coincides with the introduction of new screening requirements, suggesting these changes may be filtering for within-organization workplace relationships.

Changes implemented for this semester

Screening requirements: The Step 2B form now requires legitimate company emails and routes students through readiness checks before allowing contact submission. Students who aren't ready are directed to return later rather than submitting incomplete or low-quality contacts.

Firmer deadline: Official submission cutoff was March 6th. Proactive outreach is extending the window to March 13th for students who haven't yet submitted, but the hard stop is earlier than in previous semesters.

Rolling distribution: Surveys will launch on a rolling basis as contacts come in, rather than waiting for all submissions and distributing in large batches.

Updated materials: Revised handouts, rebuilt submission form, updated email language, and a new employee FAQ site for workplace contacts.

06 — Watch Items

Patterns warranting further review

Patterns in the Spring 2026 data that aren't necessarily problems yet but are worth monitoring as we move into distribution.

.edu emails — 15% of submissions

83 contact emails use usc.edu domains and another 10 use marshall.usc.edu. Combined with the jump in higher education orgs (6.8% → 13.1%), this could indicate legitimate USC staff contacts — or it could mean students are submitting other students or campus contacts who don't fit the study's intended participant profile. Worth cross-referencing against faculty/staff directories before distribution.

Family connections — 63% of focal employees

Roughly 43% of focal employees are immediate family members (parent, sibling, spouse). Adding extended family (6%) and family friends (14%) brings the total to about 63% of contacts coming through family channels. This is a common path for undergraduates, but it is worth considering from a data independence standpoint, particularly if the family member feels social pressure to complete the survey (which could affect response quality) or if the "workplace" being surveyed is actually a family business.

Remaining personal email contacts

Despite the new screening language, about 20% of triads still use all-personal emails. The top personal domains remain gmail.com (dominant), yahoo.com, and a handful of qq.com addresses. Based on prior semesters, these contacts may convert at lower rates. However, the overall email type distribution has shifted toward work emails relative to all prior semesters.
